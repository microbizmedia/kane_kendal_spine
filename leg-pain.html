Leg Pain Treatment in Montgomery

Leg pain can make everyday activities difficult and limit your mobility. Many patients experience discomfort that ranges from mild soreness to sharp, radiating pain. In many cases, the source of leg pain is not just the leg itself, but can also come from nerve irritation or misalignment in the spine. At Kane & Kendall Spine Management, we focus on identifying the root cause so we can provide lasting relief—not just temporary symptom management.

What Is Leg Pain?

Leg pain refers to any discomfort occurring in the lower limb, including the hip, thigh, knee, or calf. It may be caused by muscle strain, ligament injury, joint problems, or nerve-related conditions such as sciatica. In some cases, spinal misalignments can lead to pain that travels down the leg, making proper diagnosis essential.

Treatment & Prevention

Effective treatment begins with a thorough evaluation to determine the exact cause of your pain. Our approach may include spinal adjustments to correct misalignment, physical therapy to improve strength and flexibility, and soft tissue treatments to reduce tension and inflammation. We also provide guidance on posture, movement, and daily habits to help prevent future pain and support long-term recovery.

Why Choose Kane & Kendall Spine Management

Our experienced team provides personalized, hands-on care tailored to your condition. We combine advanced diagnostic techniques with proven treatment methods to help you regain mobility, reduce pain, and improve your overall quality of life in a supportive and comfortable environment.

Frequently Asked Questions

What causes leg pain?

Leg pain can be caused by muscle strain, ligament injuries, nerve irritation, joint problems, or spinal misalignment that causes pain to radiate down the leg.

When should I be concerned about leg pain?

You should seek care if your pain is severe, persistent, worsening, or accompanied by numbness, weakness, swelling, or difficulty walking.

Can back problems cause leg pain?

Yes. Conditions like nerve compression or spinal misalignment can cause pain, numbness, or tingling that travels down the leg.

What treatments are available?

Treatment may include spinal adjustments, physical therapy, soft tissue work, and lifestyle guidance depending on the cause of your pain.

How long does recovery take?

Recovery depends on the cause and severity of your condition, but early treatment often leads to faster and more effective results.

Do you accept new patients?

Yes, we welcome new patients and provide personalized care plans based on your needs and condition.
